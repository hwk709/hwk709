Verdict

🏆 —

💰 Best for Short Hold (1–3 yr)

CX-5 Preferred wins on lowest TCO at 1–3 years in most scenarios. Lower sticker means less capital at risk. Hybrid break-even usually lands at Year 4–6, so flipping before then favors the CX-5.

Exception: if the RAV4 or CX-50H trim is close in price to your CX-5 trim, the gap narrows quickly.

⚡ Best for Mid Hold (4–6 yr)

The RAV4 Hybrid typically breaks even first due to 44 MPG and the best resale in class (~18% 3-yr depreciation). Strong federal hybrid demand keeps residuals high long-term.

The CX-50 Hybrid breaks even slightly later — its 38 MPG saves ~$361/yr vs CX-5 but costs ~$1,900 more upfront.

🔋 Best for Long Hold (7–8 yr)

RAV4 Hybrid dominates at 7–8 years — fuel savings compound (~$1,496/yr vs CX-5), depreciation curve flattens, and Toyota's legendary reliability minimizes repair costs beyond warranty.

Watch the break-even timeline shift as you change trim combos — a premium RAV4 vs base CX-5 may never break even. Match trim tiers for a fair comparison.
